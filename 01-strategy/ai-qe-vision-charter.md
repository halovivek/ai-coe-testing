# AI Quality Engineering — Vision Charter

**Document owner:** Senior Director, Quality Engineering  
**Version:** 1.0 · **Status:** Approved  
**Review cycle:** Quarterly

---

## Executive summary

This charter establishes the AI Center of Excellence (CoE) for Quality Engineering — a strategic initiative to transform how the organization discovers, prevents, and predicts software defects using artificial intelligence. Over 24 months, the CoE will shift quality from a reactive function to a predictive, autonomous, and continuously learning capability embedded across every engineering team.

**Investment ask:** ₹2.4 Cr Year 1 · ₹1.8 Cr Year 2  
**Projected ROI:** 340% over 3 years  
**Primary sponsor:** CTO / VP Engineering

---

## Vision

> *"Quality is not inspected in — it is built in, predicted before release, and continuously learned from by AI systems that get smarter with every sprint."*

The AI CoE for Testing will make our organization a benchmark for AI-native quality engineering in the industry, publishing open frameworks, speaking at global conferences, and attracting top engineering talent.

---

## Mission

To design, build, and operate an AI-powered quality engineering capability that:

1. Reduces defect escape rate by 50% within 12 months
2. Cuts manual test creation effort by 50% through AI-augmented generation
3. Achieves 3× improvement in release velocity without quality trade-offs
4. Establishes self-healing automation covering 80% of the regression suite
5. Publishes a reproducible AI QE framework adopted by 3+ external organizations

---

## Strategic OKRs (Year 1)

### Objective 1: Establish AI-powered test generation at scale
- **KR1.1:** 40% of all new test cases AI-generated by M6
- **KR1.2:** Average test creation time reduced from 4h to 45min
- **KR1.3:** Requirement-to-test traceability at 95%+ via AI

### Objective 2: Achieve intelligent defect prevention
- **KR2.1:** 30% reduction in production defect escape rate by M6
- **KR2.2:** Defect prediction model accuracy >82% (F1 score)
- **KR2.3:** Shift-left: 70% of defects caught before system integration

### Objective 3: Build autonomous quality infrastructure
- **KR3.1:** Self-healing automation covers 80% of UI regression suite by M12
- **KR3.2:** Flaky test rate reduced from ~15% to <2%
- **KR3.3:** Zero manual test maintenance for covered modules

### Objective 4: Establish AI QE thought leadership
- **KR4.1:** 3 conference submissions accepted (STPCon, EuroSTAR, QA Summit)
- **KR4.2:** AI CoE GitHub repository: 500+ stars by end of Year 1
- **KR4.3:** 1 patent application filed for proprietary AI testing methodology

## Guiding principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Augment, not replace** | AI amplifies engineer capability; engineers remain accountable for quality decisions |
| **Evidence over intuition** | Every testing investment justified by data and predictive models |
| **Shift everything left** | Quality gates move upstream — design, code, PR, not just release |
| **Learn continuously** | Feedback loops ensure AI models improve with every build |
| **Ethical by design** | All AI systems tested for bias, fairness, and explainability |
| **Open by default** | Frameworks and learnings shared externally to build industry credibility |

## Risk register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| AI-generated tests miss edge cases | Medium | High | Human review gate + coverage analysis |
| Model drift in defect predictor | Low | Medium | Monthly retraining + drift monitoring |
| Engineer resistance to AI tooling | Medium | High | Change management + paired enablement |
| Vendor lock-in for AI test tools | Low | Medium | Open-source first; vendor APIs abstracted |
| Data privacy in test environments | Low | High | Synthetic data generation policy |
